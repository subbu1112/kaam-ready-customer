1. The service

Kaam Ready is a marketplace that connects customers with independent skilled workers. Workers are not our employees; they provide services independently. We facilitate discovery, booking, pricing transparency, and payment records.

2. Pricing and payment

Every service has a fixed minimum charge shown before booking. The final price is set by the worker after the work is completed, based on the actual work done, and must be approved by the customer in the app before payment. Payment is made via UPI only — directly from customer to worker. Cash payments are not permitted on the platform. A 10% platform fee applies to workers on each completed job.

3. Customer responsibilities

Provide accurate address and contact details, be present (or have an adult present) during the service, review the final price before approving, and pay promptly via UPI after approving. Report problems within 48 hours via the Report Problem option in the app.

4. Worker responsibilities

Complete identity verification (Aadhaar) before taking jobs, arrive at the agreed time, price work fairly and never below the platform minimum, confirm payments honestly, and never request cash. Repeated customer complaints, fake payment confirmations, or off-platform dealing may result in suspension.

5. Cancellations

Customers may cancel before a worker is assigned at no charge. After assignment, repeated cancellations may limit access to the service. Workers who accept and fail to show may be suspended.

6. Disputes

Price or quality disputes should be raised through the app. We will review job records, photos, and both sides' accounts, and may issue resolutions including refund recommendations or account penalties. Our role is facilitation; legal liability for the service rendered rests with the worker.

7. Limitation of liability

We verify worker identity but do not guarantee workmanship. To the maximum extent permitted by law, our liability for any claim is limited to the platform fee earned on the booking concerned.

8. Governing law

These terms are governed by the laws of India, with courts in Karnataka having jurisdiction.
